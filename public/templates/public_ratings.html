Security & Trust Ratings

What are they and how do they work?

Ratings allow organisations to assess their brand's digital trust and their public security profile. They allow third parties to ensure that their own security and privacy is respected by those they interact with.

We assess the integrity, authenticity and confidentiality of internet delivered data and communications so that users can reliably ascertain where they are going, who they are interacting with, and how confidential their interactions are. Ratings display a commitment to privacy, accountability and trustworthiness.

There are a number of industry accepted security standards that protect both internet users and service providers from malicious exploits. These standards are applicable to every organisation with an online presence regardless of size. They help to provide the foundation of a secure and private internet for everyone.

These public channels of internet communication and service delivery, by their very nature, can be assessed from the outside. Compliance to specific security standards together with Open Source Intelligence forms the basis of our Security and Trust Ratings.

Assessment Categories

As well as providing an overall rating we break down an organisation's score into three categories (and then further sub-categories). These reflect the distinct risk categories that can impact an organisation and it's third parties.

Email Security

Can we trust email communications to and from an organisation? Are they encrypted, private and secure?

Email is inherently insecure. It may surprise you to learn just how insecure it really is. For example, did you know that messages which you thought were deleted years ago may be sitting on servers half-way around the world? Or that your messages can be read and modified in transit, even before they reach their destination? Or even that the username and password that you use to login to your email servers can be stolen and used by hackers?

It's no surprise that 95% of all hacking attacks and data breaches involve email. Yet critically more than 67% of organisations have not fully implemented readily available email authentication solutions.

We check for the implementation of security standards that protect a domain from email fraud such as Phishing, CEO Fraud, Invoice Fraud, Business Email Compromise, Brand abuse and scams.

We check that emails are protected from interception by third parties. Without this protection messages are as open to snoopers as a postcard in the mail.

Web Security

Can we trust the website to be encrypted, private and secure?

Communications travel across a complex network of networks in order to get from point A to point B. Throughout that journey they are susceptible to interception by unintended recipients who know how to manipulate the networks. Sites that don't comply to encryption security standards are crippling the privacy controls you thought were protecting your data.

Unencrypted websites can be used to do more than just steal sensitive information that a user might submit. An attacker can take advantage of the lack of encryption to inject malware into a website, which can lead to the complete compromise of a user’s computer and all of their data. We check to ensure the integrity of data transmission to ensure privacy and security.

Web Application Security

Can we trust that the website is protected from common vulnerabilities?

We check for vulnerabilities some of which are among those listed in the "Ten Most Critical Web Application Security Risks" published by The Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP). These vulnerabilities can put an organisation and website visitors equally at risk. The implementation of security standards protect you against the types of attacks that your site is most likely to come across, such as cross-site scripting (XSS), man-in-the-middle (MitM) attacks, click-jacking, cross-site request forgery and other threats.

Infrastructure

Is the public facing infrastructure already compromised or vulnerable to common security issues?

Checks ensure that an organisations IP address and domain is not infected with malware, bots, exploit scanners, spyware, hositle spiders and more. We check a webserver for common vulnerabilities that can easily be exploited. CVE vulnerabilities are identified by passive fingerprinting of server software and version, open ports are identified to ensure there is a minimum number of exposed services.

We use data from numerous security intelligence feeds, intrusion detection feeds and a system of automated bots, crawlers and honeypots which continuously collect data from across the Internet.

The cyber hygiene gap is created by those who are unaware of, or who neglect, security best practices.

Non-intrusive Scanning & Open Source Intelligence

As ratings are generated through regular scans of externally observable data and analysis of Open Source Intelligence they can be used to assess the security and trust profile of third parties. Some of the most devastating breaches in the past few years have been rooted in the security weaknesses of third parties, and in fact, hackers themselves admit that contractors are often their primary target. This makes our ratings perfect for Vendor Risk Management and Cyber Insurance risk assessments.

Service FAQ’s

  • How accurate are your ratings?
    We have taken great effort to make sure that our ratings are accurate and transparent. We have focused on assessing data points that are tangible and clearly quantifiable. That are as relevant to multinational corporations as they are to a small startup.

    Our ratings explicitly indicate whether an organisation has sufficiently implemented security standards that allow us to trust our email and web based interactions with them. Their public security and trust profile.

    Whilst these can correlate to the internal security profile of an organisation, we should not assume that an "A" rating definitively means that an organisation is fully secure.

    There are many aspects that contribute to the security of an online service or organisation that are not externally available and would require active testing.

    Our ratings provide a useful tool for self and third party assessments. Based on quantifiable data they are an improvement on, or add tremendous value to, self report security questionnaires currently used when assessing third party risk.
  • How can you say my organisation cannot be trusted?

    In the global digital economy we can interact with hundreds of organisations a day. We cannot assess the internal security controls an organisations, however we can assess their compliance to industry accepted security standards with reference to internet based communications. These standards specifically exist to promote integrity, authenticity and confidentially.

    Without compliance to these standards, or if an organisation has vulnerable or compromised infrastructure, we can safely say the we cannot wholly trust our email and/or web communications to be secure, authentic and confidential.

    These cyber security risks impact digital avenues of communication and thus are not just an internal security issue. They impact all that might communicate with an organisation.

    Implementation of these standards and the basic cyber hygiene that would result in a "trusted" rating are fundamental to even a minimal cyber security compliance initiative.

  • How long will it take to go from a bad to good rating?
    Implementation of security standards and remediation of vulnerabilities will have an immediate security impact on your organisation. It is possible to go from a bad to a good rating almost overnight. Commonly it might take 30 days to implement and test remediation depending on the current level of compliance.
